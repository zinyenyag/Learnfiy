# Zimbabwe Commercial Subjects Syllabus Reference
**Forms 5-6 (2024-2030)**

## ACCOUNTING SYLLABUS

### Topics Covered:

#### TOPIC 1: FINANCIAL ACCOUNTING
- Introduction to Accounting and Business Ethics
  - Users of accounting information and their information needs
  - Business Ethics applications
  - Types of business organisations (formal, non-formal)
  - Branches of accounting (Financial, Financial Reporting, Cost, Management)

- Accounting Concepts
  - Historical cost convention
  - Money measurement concept
  - Substance over form
  - Realisation concept
  - Prudence concept
  - Matching (accruals) concept
  - Materiality concept
  - Consistency concept
  - Going concern concept

- Double Entry up to Trial Balance
  - Accounts for expenses and income (prepayments and accruals)
  - Accounts of non-current assets, liabilities and capital
  - Accounts of disposals on non-current assets
  - Accounts of provision for depreciation
  - Accounts for bad debts
  - Accounts of allowances for doubtful debts
  - Trial balance

- Trial Balance and Errors
  - Errors which do not affect trial balance
  - Errors which affect trial balance and suspense accounts
  - Correction of errors through journal proper
  - Preparation of suspense accounts
  - Preparation of corrected trial balances
  - Correction of gross profit, net profit/loss, working capital

- Control Applications
  - Adjustments to control accounts and personal accounts
  - Reconciliation of personal ledger balances to control account balances
  - Updated cash book
  - Bank reconciliation statements
  - Treatment of bank overdraft
  - Errors in cash book and bank statements

- Valuation of Assets
  - Inventory valuation (IAS2)
  - Inventory valuation basis: Cost or net realisable value
  - Valuation methods: Perpetual and periodic, FIFO, Weighted Average Cost (AVCO)
  - Non-current assets valuation (IAS16)
  - Non-current assets valuation methods: Replacement cost, Disposal/exit value

- Financial Statements (IAS1)
  - Statements of profit and loss and other comprehensive income for sole proprietors, partnerships, companies
  - Statements of changes in equity of companies
  - Statements of financial position for various business entities

#### TOPIC 2: FINANCIAL REPORTING
- Financial statement analysis and interpretation
- Ratio analysis
- Cash flow statements

#### TOPIC 3: COST ACCOUNTING
- Elements of cost (Direct materials, Direct labour, Overheads)
- Cost classification (Fixed, Variable, Semi-variable)
- Overhead allocation and apportionment
- Absorption costing vs Marginal costing
- Job and Batch Costing
  - Prime cost calculation
  - Overhead absorption
  - Total cost and selling price
- Standard Costing
  - Setting standards
  - Types of standards
  - Variance analysis (Material, Labour, Sales variances)
  - Reconciliation statements

#### TOPIC 4: MANAGEMENT ACCOUNTING
- Budgeting and Budgetary Control
  - Importance of budgeting
  - Behavioral aspects of budgeting
  - Types of budgets (Sales, Production, Purchases, Cash, Master)
  - Limiting factors
  - Flexed budget statements
  - Variance analysis between actual and flexed budgets

- Investment Appraisal
  - Accounting Rate of Return (ARR)
  - Payback Period
  - Net Present Value (NPV)
  - Internal Rate of Return (IRR)
  - Advantages and disadvantages of each technique

### Assessment Structure:
- **School Based Continuous Assessment (SBCA)**: 20%
  - One project per form
  - Project stages: Problem Identification, Investigation, Solution Generation, Selection, Refinement, Presentation, Evaluation
  
- **Summative Assessment (SA)**: 80%
  - Paper 1: Multiple choice (1h 20min, 40 marks, 15%)
  - Paper 2: Structured questions (1h 45min, 100 marks, 25%)
  - Paper 3: Problem-solving (2h 30min, 100 marks, 40%)
